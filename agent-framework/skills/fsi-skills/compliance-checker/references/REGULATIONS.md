# Financial Regulatory Reference

## Key Regulations

### Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)
- Requires financial institutions to assist government agencies in detecting and preventing money laundering
- Institutions must file CTRs for cash transactions over $10,000
- SARs must be filed for suspicious transactions

### USA PATRIOT Act
- Enhanced BSA requirements for customer identification
- Requires Customer Identification Programs (CIP)
- Mandates enhanced due diligence for correspondent banking

### Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Framework
- Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements
- Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Rule
- Beneficial Ownership Requirements

### FATF Recommendations
- 40 Recommendations for combating money laundering
- Risk-based approach to AML/CFT
- International cooperation standards

## Reporting Requirements

| Report | Trigger | Filing Deadline |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| CTR | Cash > $10,000 | 15 days |
| SAR | Suspicious activity | 30 days (60 if no suspect identified) |
| CMIR | Currency/monetary instruments > $10,000 across border | At time of transport |

## Risk Categories

### High-Risk Customers
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Non-resident customers
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Money Service Businesses (MSBs)

### High-Risk Geographies
- FATF Black List (Call for Action)
- FATF Grey List (Increased Monitoring)
- OFAC sanctioned countries
